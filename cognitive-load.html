Cognitive Load Theory (CLT)

Cognitive Load Theory helps explain why learners struggle when too much information is presented at once. In video learning, overload can happen because the stream is continuous and time-limited.

Types of cognitive load (simple classroom-friendly description)

  • Intrinsic load: complexity of the content itself (e.g., multi-step procedures).
  • Extraneous load: unnecessary burden caused by poor design (e.g., distracting music, irrelevant images).
  • Germane load: effort devoted to building useful understanding (schemas).

CTML principles can be seen as design rules to reduce extraneous load and support germane processing.
