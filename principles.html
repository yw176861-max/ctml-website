12 Multimedia Learning Principles

Below is a practical summary for designing educational videos.

  1. Coherence: remove irrelevant sounds, images, and words.
  2. Signaling: highlight key ideas using cues (arrows, headings, emphasis).
  3. Redundancy: avoid presenting identical printed text with narration and graphics at the same time.
  4. Spatial Contiguity: place related text and visuals near each other.
  5. Temporal Contiguity: present corresponding narration and visuals together.
  6. Segmenting: break long explanations into learner-paced chunks.
  7. Pre-training: teach key terms/components before complex processes.
  8. Modality: prefer narration + visuals over onscreen text + visuals for complex content.
  9. Multimedia: words + pictures are better than words alone.
  10. Personalization: use conversational style instead of overly formal style.
  11. Voice: human voice is generally more effective than machine voice.
  12. Image: a talking head is not always necessary; show it only if it helps learning.

In your video design, try to apply at least 4–6 principles explicitly and explain why you used them.
