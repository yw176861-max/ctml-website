Key Elements (Assumptions)

1) Dual Channels

Learners process information through two main channels: visual/pictorial and auditory/verbal.

2) Limited Capacity

Each channel can handle only a limited amount of information at one time.

3) Active Processing

Meaningful learning happens when learners actively select relevant information, organize it into a structure, and integrate it with prior knowledge.

These assumptions explain why “more information” can sometimes reduce learning, especially in fast-paced videos. Good design helps learners focus on what matters and reduces overload.
