What is CTML?

The Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (CTML) explains how learners build understanding from words (spoken or printed) and pictures (graphics, animations, video). It emphasizes designing multimedia to support meaningful learning, not just adding more media.

Core idea

People learn better when multimedia is designed to guide attention, manage cognitive load, and support active processing (selecting, organizing, integrating).

In educational videos

  • Use visuals that directly support the narration.
  • Keep the pace manageable and segment complex explanations.
  • Avoid unnecessary decorations that distract from the learning goal.
