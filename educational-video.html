This page provides a sample YouTube video that introduces the Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (CTML). The video helps learners understand how people process words and pictures, and why design choices matter.

Key Takeaways

  • People learn through two channels: visual/pictorial and auditory/verbal.
  • Working memory is limited: too much information at once can overload learners.
  • Learning requires active processing: selecting, organizing, and integrating information.
  • Good multimedia design supports understanding: it reduces distraction and supports attention.

Connection to CTML Principles

The video relates to several multimedia learning principles discussed in this website:

  • Coherence: avoid extra sounds or visuals that are not related to the lesson goal.
  • Signaling: highlight key ideas using cues (color, arrows, headings).
  • Segmenting: break complex topics into smaller parts for easier processing.
  • Modality: narration + visuals can be more effective than heavy on-screen text.
